Colophon · how this was built

Design notes, in the same font size as the claims.

This page is unlinked from the main navigation. It documents how the site was designed and built — four generations, including the concepts that lost — so that anyone who wants to work this way can.

Who made this

This site was designed and built end-to-end by Claude (Fable 5, Anthropic) working from the product's own documents and evidence files: generations one to three in a single autonomous overnight session on 12 July 2026, and the fourth — this one — in a daytime session the same day, briefed to challenge the keeper from outside its own aesthetic lineage. Frixos Andreou wrote the briefs and judges the results; every number on the site comes from Sentys's published benchmark reports, and the design was never allowed to touch them.

Start from what the product is

Sentys is an interoceptive nervous system for machines: it listens to raw sensor streams, learns what "healthy" feels like without labels, and speaks up in plain language when something drifts. The audience is maintenance and reliability engineers — people who trust instruments and distrust decoration. Every aesthetic decision had to survive both facts.

Generations one to three: the Resonance lineage

The first bake-off (prototypes preserved under /concepts/a, /concepts/b, /concepts/c) was won by the Chladni plate: dust on a vibrating surface settling into the standing-wave pattern of its resonant mode — what "a machine's feeling of healthy" literally looks like in physics.

A · "Afferent"LOST

An anatomy-atlas plate of a centrifugal pump with afferent nerve fibres converging on a ganglion — the "missing organ" thesis drawn literally. The purest conceptual match, but the nerve drawing read as a sparse network diagram, and the direction drifted toward a moody dark-AI-site cliché.

B · "Recorder"LOST

The whole page as a chart recorder: live pen trace on sprocket-holed paper, typed red-pen annotations, a rubber stamp. Instantly credible for plant engineers — but as a whole-site skin it was print nostalgia that fought the advanced-motion brief. Its instrument furniture survived into later generations (and its central idea returns, transformed, in v4).

C · "Resonance"WON

The Chladni plate: physically true for vibration sensing, the strongest live-motion ceiling, the clearest one-visual statement of the product — healthy is a stable pattern; drift deforms and heats it.

v2, "the continuous instrument," put the plate behind the entire page, conducted by scroll: every section declared a resonant mode, and the centerpiece became One engine's life — Engine 03's real 179-cycle history scrubbed by scroll, the plate heating as the rotation sense crossed 3× at cycle 123.

v3, "the instrument sleeps," fixed what v2 broke: a background that never goes quiet is wrong about a product whose pitch is that silence is the default (122 quiet cycles; one false alarm in thirty engines). The plate froze in every reading section and woke exactly twice. Its system: Clash Display / Supreme / IBM Plex Mono; graphite #0f1216 and dust #ece5d6 bands with one ember accent #f0a43c; the hero a single-pass WebGL Chladni shader with a typed sense-voice line. v3 is the incumbent this generation was built to challenge — it lives on branch redesign-v3.

v4: the annunciator — a challenger from outside the lineage

Generation four began with a hard rule: the Resonance lineage (graphite + ember, standing waves) was closed, and the daylight drafting-sheet axis was already rejected (see concepts/redline/). Three concepts from unclaimed territory fought a second single-fold bake-off:

vitals (/concepts/vitals) — the machine as patient, literally: clinical blue-white, Unit 03's real surprise trace drawn as an ECG strip with event flags at cycles 123 and 179. Lost because above the fold it collapses into generic light health-SaaS — the identity hangs entirely on one chart, and the audience is plant engineers, not clinicians.

sonar (/concepts/sonar) — the deep listening: petrol abyss, a live waterfall spectrogram with a defect tone slowly forming, phosphor cyan. Lost because it is a third dark site in a row — beside v3 they read as siblings — and it grazes the stock "near-black + one acid accent" look. The waterfall reads as aurora, not instrument.

annunciator (/concepts/annunciator) — the winner. The site as the plant's own control console: pebble-grey enamel fascia, engraved legend plates, corner screws, and an annunciator window grid in which exactly one window burns amber — SURPRISE HIGH · UNIT 03 · CYCLE 123. It won on the brief's own criterion: credibility with maintenance and reliability engineers. Annunciator windows, LAMP TEST and ACKNOWLEDGE buttons are furniture those engineers touch every shift, and the signature does argument work — the lit window is the product: a warning light for what hasn't failed yet.

The v4 design system — "THE PANEL"

Materials, not backgrounds. Three materials carry the whole page: enamel (RAL-7032 pebble grey #c8c5ba, brushed, grooved, screwed — the structure), dark glass (#211f1a instrument bays: the chart recorder, the live demo, LED readouts #5ce08c behind smoked windows), and paper (#fbf9f2 — the work order, the findings report, the one-page ask, this page). The brand's ember #f0a43c appears as a lamp, not a paint: the lit annunciator lens, deepened to #a4650a for engraved text on enamel.

Type. Tanker for display (one weight, heavy, industrial — the engraved fascia legend); Khand for small legends, tags and buttons (condensed, letterspaced caps — the engraved label plate); Supreme for body prose; IBM Plex Mono for anything that is data. All self-hosted woff2 (Fontshare / IBM), zero external requests.

The signatures. One per fold, engineered not illustrated: the hero's annunciator grid (CSS only — backlit lenses, blink, a working LAMP TEST and ACKNOWLEDGE), and the story's chart recorder — Engine 03's real 179-cycle life replayed as a pen-recorder feed on chart paper (canvas, log scale, the run's actual amber threshold, the warning and failure stamped on the paper as the pen reaches them, RUN/HOLD controls). Generation one's losing "Recorder" concept, returned as a single instrument in an enamel console.

Motion budget: the blinking window, the recorder feed, the live demo. Everything else holds still — this is still the company whose product stays silent for 122 cycles.

Evidence is design material

The chart recorder is not an illustration. It replays Engine 03 of the NASA C-MAPSS demo fleet from demo/fleet_data.json — the same real model output the live demo plays — with the actual amber threshold (3.0×) from the run and the actual first-warning cycle (123). The hero's LED voice line types the demo's verbatim triage sentence. The rule for the whole site: every number comes from the published evidence files; where a pre-registered gate missed its bar, that is shown too (see the FEMTO and XJTU plates, stamped "BAR MISSED — PUBLISHED"). Honesty is the brand, so the design leans into it instead of hiding it.

Craft floor

  • Reduced motion: prefers-reduced-motion stops the blink, the typing line and the reveals, and draws the recorder chart complete — all 179 cycles, both event stamps, every stage note visible.
  • No JS: content is never hidden behind script; reveals only ever add opacity.
  • Performance: no WebGL, no framework — one canvas that only runs while on screen; self-hosted fonts preloaded; demo iframe and images lazy; zero external requests.
  • Accessibility: skip link, focus-visible styles, aria-labelled annunciator and chart, engraved text held ≥ 4.5:1 on enamel, keyboard-operable LAMP TEST.

How to reproduce the method

1. Read the product's own documents until the design direction is forced
   by the identity (not chosen from a trend).
2. Build 3 genuinely different single-fold prototypes. Real type, real
   palette, real signature technique — no lorem, no grey boxes.
3. Screenshot desktop + mobile (headless Chrome / puppeteer), judge with
   fresh eyes against identity + audience, pick one. Keep the losers.
4. Build the full site from the winner. Then iterate: screenshot →
   critique → revise, at least three passes, checking overflow, contrast,
   console errors, reduced-motion, and mobile every pass.
5. Let real data be the ornament. If the product has evidence, chart the
   evidence — never a stock illustration of it.
6. When a design wins, close its lineage. The next generation must attack
   from territory the last one never claimed.
