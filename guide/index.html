Colophon · how this was built

Design notes, in the same font size as the claims.

This page is unlinked from the main navigation. It documents how the site was designed and built — including the concepts that lost — so that anyone who wants to work this way can.

Who made this

This site was designed and built end-to-end by Claude (Fable 5, Anthropic) in a single autonomous overnight session on 12 July 2026 — concept exploration, design system, WebGL shader, copy, accessibility, brand kit, and these notes, working from the product's own documents and evidence files. Frixos Andreou wrote the brief and merged the result; every number on the site comes from Sentys's published benchmark reports, and the design was not allowed to touch them.

Start from what the product is

Sentys is an interoceptive nervous system for machines: it listens to raw sensor streams, learns what "healthy" feels like without labels, and speaks up in plain language when something drifts. The audience is maintenance and reliability engineers — people who trust instruments and distrust decoration. Every aesthetic decision had to survive both facts.

The design direction was derived from the product, not applied to it: vibration made visible. A Chladni plate — dust on a vibrating surface settling into the standing-wave pattern of its resonant mode — is what "a machine's feeling of healthy" literally looks like in physics. A stable pattern is health; a deforming, heating pattern is drift. That is the hero, and it is also the product's pitch, wordlessly.

The bake-off: three concepts, two losers

Three genuinely different single-fold hero prototypes were built and screenshotted at desktop and mobile widths, then judged with fresh eyes against the product identity and the audience. The losing prototypes are preserved, working, under /concepts/a, /concepts/b and /concepts/c.

A · "Afferent"LOST

An anatomy-atlas plate of a centrifugal pump with afferent nerve fibres converging on a ganglion — the "missing organ" thesis drawn literally. The purest conceptual match, and the serif plate-typography was lovely. It lost on execution ceiling: the nerve drawing read as a sparse network diagram rather than an organ, and the whole direction drifted toward a moody dark-AI-site cliché that would need illustration-grade drawing to escape.

B · "Recorder"LOST

The whole page as a chart recorder: live pen trace on sprocket-holed chart paper, typed red-pen annotations, a rubber stamp. Instantly credible for plant engineers and the most distinctive of the three — but as a whole-site skin it is a print-nostalgia direction that fights the advanced-motion brief, and its signature (the pen) didn't even land in the fold. Its instrument furniture survived into the winner: the mono readouts, the threshold annotation on the evidence chart, the alarm line "set from healthy data alone".

C · "Resonance"WON

The Chladni plate. Physically true for vibration sensing (engineers recognise it as physics, not decoration), the strongest live-motion ceiling, and the clearest one-visual statement of the product: healthy is a stable pattern; drift deforms it and heats it. Its known risks — text legibility over the pattern, centered-hero cliché — were fixed by composition: an asymmetric layout with a directional scrim, and the pattern's visual mass held right of the text column.

v2: the continuous instrument

After a first full build, the founder pushed back: the visual concept was new, but the page skeleton was inherited from the old site. v2 answered that. The Chladni plate became a fixed layer behind the entire page, conducted by scroll: every section declares a resonant mode and the plate morphs between them as you travel. Light backgrounds were removed; light now exists only as paper, the documents Sentys actually produces. And the centerpiece became One engine's life: Engine 03's real 179-cycle history scrubbed by scroll, with the plate's drift driven by the engine's actual surprise value at the cycle under your finger. When the rotation sense crosses 3× at cycle 123, the room itself starts to heat.

v3: the instrument sleeps

v2 solved the skeleton and created a new problem: the plate never rested, and a background that never goes quiet is wrong about this product — Sentys' pitch is that silence is the default (122 quiet cycles; one false alarm in thirty engines). v3's rule: the plate is frozen in every reading section (the render loop literally stops) and wakes exactly twice — the hero's drift events and the story as the engine sickens. The story also learned manners: the chartbox visibly docks with an ember pulse and a "scroll to run" hint, a progress rail maps the wheel to cycles 0–179 with a tick at the first warning, the playhead has mass, and a focus vignette holds the room while you drive. Decoration budget: three loud moments (hero, story, the paper ask-sheet); everything else went quiet.

Fig. 01: the loser, salvaged

After v3 shipped, the founder asked whether Concept A's hero — the one he "envisioned but couldn't put to words" — could be saved. Its verdict had said it lost on execution ceiling: escaping the network-diagram look "would need illustration-grade drawing". That ceiling no longer exists. The engraved pump in Fig. 01 — the missing organ is AI-generated (Higgsfield, Nano Banana; prompted as a 19th-century atlas plate in the site's ivory-on-graphite palette), and the living part — afferent fibres, travelling pulses, a breathing ganglion — is the original concept's hand-written canvas code, re-skinned from bioluminescent teal to dust and ember, senses renamed to the product's real ones. Hybrid was the unlock: the image model supplies the organ-grade drawing a canvas can't, the canvas supplies the life an image can't. Two rules keep it honest: the section is quiet (its surprise event fires once per visit, then the organ rests), and centered type is used here as punctuation — single short lines over darkness — while the hero keeps its asymmetric composition.

The design system

Type

Clash Display for display (wide, technical, quietly confident), Supreme for body, IBM Plex Mono for anything that is data: eyebrows, readouts, thresholds, work orders. All self-hosted woff2 (Fontshare / IBM), zero external requests.

Color

Dark "plate" bands (#0f1216 graphite, #ece5d6 dust) carry the instrument: hero, demo, evidence, founder. Light "porcelain" bands (#f2efe8 with a faint 48px chart-grid texture) carry the reading matter. One accent — ember (#f0a43c, deepened to #8a5c0a on light for contrast) — because amber-before-red is the product. Green and red appear only as data semantics in charts and status stamps.

The signature

The hero is a single-pass WebGL fragment shader of the Chladni plate function cos(mπx)cos(nπy) − cos(nπx)cos(mπy): dust accumulates where the standing wave is still (the nodal lines), the plate slowly morphs between resonant modes, the cursor adds a local ripple. Every 16 seconds a drift event warps the pattern from one corner and heats the dust ember-to-red — while a mono "sense-voice" line under the CTAs types the product's actual triage sentence: "vibration: 3.2× healthy baseline; temperature and flow within range." The two drift messages are verbatim artifacts from the benchmark runs, not invented copy.

Evidence is design material

The chart in the evidence section is not an illustration. It replays Engine 03 of the NASA C-MAPSS demo fleet from demo/fleet_data.json — the same real model output the live demo plays — on a log₂ scale, with the actual amber threshold (3.0×) from the run and the actual first-warning cycle (123). The rule for the whole site: every number comes from the published evidence files; where a pre-registered gate missed its bar, that is shown too (see the FEMTO and XJTU cards, stamped "BAR MISSED — PUBLISHED"). Honesty is the brand, so the design leans into it instead of hiding it.

Craft floor

  • Reduced motion: prefers-reduced-motion removes the shader (a static gradient stands in), the typing caret, reveals, and chart animation — the chart draws complete.
  • No JS: content is never hidden; reveals are gated on an html.js class.
  • Performance: one shader pass at devicePixelRatio ≤ 1.5, paused when offscreen or the tab is hidden; self-hosted fonts preloaded; demo iframe lazy; zero external requests.
  • Accessibility: skip link, focus-visible styles, aria-labelled canvas chart, contrast held ≥ 4.5:1 for text on both bands.

How to reproduce the method

1. Read the product's own documents until the design direction is forced
   by the identity (not chosen from a trend).
2. Build 3 genuinely different single-fold prototypes. Real type, real
   palette, real signature technique — no lorem, no grey boxes.
3. Screenshot desktop + mobile (headless Chrome / puppeteer), judge with
   fresh eyes against identity + audience, pick one. Keep the losers.
4. Build the full site from the winner. Then iterate: screenshot →
   critique → revise, at least three passes, checking overflow, contrast,
   console errors, reduced-motion, and mobile every pass.
5. Let real data be the ornament. If the product has evidence, chart the
   evidence — never a stock illustration of it.
